# Lead Qualification: Summit Dental Care

**Website:** https://www.summitdentalcare.ie

## Company Overview

Summit Dental Care is a family and cosmetic dental practice located in Galway City, Ireland. Established in 2012, the practice serves approximately 3,000 active patients across general dentistry, cosmetic procedures (veneers, whitening, Invisalign), and emergency dental care.

The team consists of 12 staff including three dentists (Dr. Aiden Murphy, Dr. Sarah Brennan, Dr. Conor Lyons), two dental hygienists, a practice manager (Eileen Fogarty), and six administrative and nursing staff. The practice operates from a single location on Eyre Square and recently expanded into an adjacent unit to accommodate a third treatment room.

Summit positions itself as a premium provider. Their pricing for cosmetic procedures (Invisalign from €3,500, veneers from €800 per tooth) sits above the Galway average. The practice has a steady patient base but is actively looking to grow in the cosmetic segment, particularly among 25–40 year olds.

## Current Web Presence

**Website:** https://www.summitdentalcare.ie
**Platform:** WordPress 6.2 (Twenty Twenty-One theme, no child theme)
**Quality Assessment:** Functional but significantly dated. The site appears to have been built around 2018 and has received minimal updates since.

**Homepage:** Features a full-width stock photo hero, a brief "About Us" section, and a list of treatments as plain text links. No calls-to-action above the fold. The navigation menu contains 14 top-level items, many of which lead to thin content pages (less than 100 words each).

**Mobile Experience:** Poor. Text overlaps on the "Treatments" and "Our Team" pages. The appointment request form fields are misaligned on screens under 400px width. The phone number is not tap-to-call. The "Book Now" button exists but is positioned below the third content fold on mobile.

**Performance:** Page load time measured at 6.2 seconds on mobile (Lighthouse, 4G simulation). The primary bottleneck is unoptimised hero images (1.2MB JPEG on the homepage alone). No lazy loading is implemented. No caching plugin is active.

**SEO:** Meta descriptions are missing on 8 of 11 indexed pages. The title tags use the default WordPress format ("Page Name | Summit Dental Care"). No structured data (Schema.org) is implemented. The last blog post was published in March 2024.

**Booking Integration:** No online booking system. The "Book Appointment" page links to a Google Form with basic fields (name, email, phone, preferred date). The form does not integrate with their practice management software.

**Security:** SSL certificate is active on most pages but returns a mixed content warning on the appointment request page (loading a non-HTTPS Google Form embed).

**Social Media:** Facebook page has 1,200 followers but the last post was December 2024. Instagram account has 340 followers, last post November 2024. Neither platform is linked from the website.

**Google Business:** Profile exists and is reasonably maintained. 87 reviews with an average of 4.2 stars. Q&A section has 5 unanswered questions dating back to 2024. The profile is not linked from the website.

## Fit Assessment

**Score:** 8/10
**Verdict:** STRONG

**Reasoning:**
- **Need Signal:** High — The website has clear, measurable problems that directly impact patient acquisition. A 6+ year old site with broken mobile experience, no online booking, 6-second load times, and zero SEO maintenance represents significant technical debt. These are not cosmetic issues — they are revenue issues.
- **Size Fit:** High — 12 employees, established practice since 2012, recent physical expansion (third treatment room). Large enough to justify a professional redesign budget but small enough that a freelancer can handle the project without an agency overhead structure.
- **Budget Signal:** Medium-High — Premium pricing model on cosmetic procedures suggests willingness to invest in quality. The recent physical expansion indicates available capital and a growth mindset. However, no explicit budget has been discussed, and dental practices can be cost-conscious on non-clinical spending.
- **Accessibility:** High — Practice manager (Eileen Fogarty) is listed on Google Business and is likely the decision maker for marketing/web decisions. Active phone number and email on every page. No gatekeeper layers apparent.
- **Timing Signal:** Medium — No explicit "we're looking for a web designer" signal found. However, the abandoned social media accounts, unanswered Google Business questions, and 2+ year content gap suggest they know things need attention. The recent expansion may be the trigger — they've invested in physical capacity and may now be looking at digital capacity.

## Key Findings

- WordPress site on the default Twenty Twenty-One theme with no child theme or custom CSS beyond a logo swap
- Mobile experience is broken on at least 3 pages (treatments, team, appointment form) — text overlap and misaligned form fields
- No online booking integration despite being a service-based business where appointment scheduling is the primary conversion goal
- Google Business profile has 87 reviews (4.2★) but is not linked or displayed on the website
- SSL mixed content warning on the appointment page — potential security concern that may deter patients
- Homepage hero image is 1.2MB unoptimised — no lazy loading, no caching plugin, no image compression
- 8 of 11 indexed pages missing meta descriptions, no Schema.org markup
- Last blog post: March 2024 (over 2 years of content silence)
- Social media abandoned: Facebook (last post Dec 2024), Instagram (last post Nov 2024)
- 5 unanswered questions on Google Business profile, some dating back 10+ months
- Two competitor dental practices in Galway (Galway Dental Clinic, Westport Dental) have modern, booking-enabled websites with active content strategies
- The Google Form booking page is a poor user experience — no date picker, no treatment selection, no confirmation

## Recommendation

Strong prospect. The website has clear, measurable problems that directly impact patient acquisition and the practice's ability to convert its growing cosmetic segment. The approach should lead with the mobile experience and online booking gap — these are revenue issues, not cosmetic ones. A modern, fast, mobile-first site with integrated booking could measurably increase new patient inquiries, particularly from the 25–40 demographic they're targeting for cosmetic work. The practice's recent expansion suggests they're in an investment mindset. Aim for a discovery call with the practice manager (Eileen Fogarty).
